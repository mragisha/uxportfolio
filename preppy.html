Preppy
A SOCIAL MEAL PREP APP

Overview

Hi-fidelity wirefames | UX | 12 weeks

While taking the course of Foundation in HCDE, during my first semester, our team
pondered on few problem areas we are witnessing these days. And the idea of home-
cooked meals hit us, which we found 90% of young adults are facing these days. Living
away from home, either working or studying young adults are getting away from the home-
cooked healthy meals. Because of eating unhealthy food out, they are suffering from some
chronic diseases as well.

So, here we have our problem statement- Encouraging young adults to eat more home-
cooked food

MY ROLE

Contextual Inquiry, Persona, Affinity Diagram, Ideation, Sketching, Wireframes and
Prototype

Process

CONTEXTUAL INQUIRY

Now that we had a problem statement, we wanted to know more about the behavior, pain
points and thoughts of the millennial population out there.
We chose contextual inquiry for our user research and our friends, cousins and neighbors
as our users. As a team of two with a rough script, we joined them at their homes when
they cook their next meal.

We asked open ended questions about their day, cooking pattern, emotions related to
cooking and about other areas which revolve around cooking like grocery shopping,
required skills, devices, etc.

PERSONAS AND SCENARIOS

Learning about the users and consolidating the points from the contextual inquiry, we
prepared 3 personas and user scenario. These were based on three major kinds of
problems our users were facing related to food and health-

-Always on go, no time to sit and eat peacefully,
- Having serious health issues and no time to cook
- Mindful eater and loves socializing in free time

AFFINITY DIAGRAM

We started listening to the recorded user interviews and noting down the important points
om colored sticky notes.

Now for a better understanding of the problems and to facilitate our ideation process, we
segregated the points and prepared affinity diagrams.

There were 16 areas where ours users showed concern and almost all of them revolved
around three major areas, namely, Feelings with cooking, Time and Technique

HOW MIGHT WE???

Now that we were all on the same page and knew the major problem areas we were
targeting, we started brainstorming. For a facilitating more and more ideas, we started with
HMW- HOW MIGHT WE??

IDEATION AND SKETCHING

With all the HMWs right on front of us, each of us silently started and pick one of a bunch
of those and put the ideas on paper. All kind of crazy ideas were welcomed, with no
judgement on the quality but just quantity.

Several rounds of sketching, presenting and voting went through for hours until we could
come up with the most voted, feasible and overall the best one. During this 10+10 method,
we were under no-constraints and were open for all kind of product ideas (software or
hardware, new or an improvement.)

Social meal prep was the idea that stood out among the tons of other ideas. Now all of our
heads started the process of making this one more and more useful, keeping our personas
in mind and solving most of their problems.

For this, rounds and rounds of sketching, presenting and voting went through as part of the
brainstorming.

PROTOTYPING

With the final ideas and sketches ready, we took to Adobe XD for prototyping. We
prototype the solution without worrying about the visual design, color and animation.

We prepared vertical prototype focusing more on a smooth flow rather than random
features. There were three iterations of prototyping and testing.

Version 1

Version 2

USABILITY TESTING

We created task sheets, google form to make a note of everything. The users were given
the app prototype on a phone, we made use of Adobe XD mobile app for this. As the users
were operating, we were silently observing them listening as they were thinking aloud.

Two observers were deployed for the process for each user, one would take detailed notes
and other one silently observing and answering to user's queries.

FINAL VERSION

As per the observation and the results from testing, we improved the prototype to the final
version. We made changes in the features, visibility, iconography and making it look more
consistent.

Conclusion

The process went really smooth except for the constraint of limited time and deadlines of
deliverables as a course project. We wished we could have recruited the users well and
could have covered a diverse set of users for interviews.

But the current idea was well focused to a limited set of users and we observed that they
loved the overall idea.
